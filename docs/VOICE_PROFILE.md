# MAW Labs — Voice Profile
# Reference before generating any content for mawlabs.ai

---

## WHO READS THIS

Anyone writing content for MAW Labs: blog posts, social copy, website copy, email outreach, case studies.

---

## CORE VOICE

**Direct. Analytical. Specific.**

Sounds like a builder explaining their work to another builder — not a marketer explaining a product to a buyer.

The voice assumes the reader is smart. It doesn't oversell. It doesn't explain what AI is. It shows what was built, how it worked, and what the outcome was.

---

## WHAT TO ALWAYS DO

- **Use specific numbers.** "Reduced document review from 6 hours to 45 minutes" beats "significantly reduced processing time."
- **Name the thing.** File names, API endpoints, model names, line counts. Specificity is credibility.
- **Lead with the outcome.** Don't bury the result at the end of a paragraph.
- **Show the work.** How it was built, what broke, what we'd do differently.
- **Write short sentences.** One idea per sentence. No dependent clauses stacked three deep.

---

## WHAT TO NEVER DO

Never use these words or phrases:

- "excited to share" / "thrilled to announce"
- "deep dive" / "let's dive in"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "paradigm shift"
- "unlock" (as in "unlock your potential")
- "transform your workflow" / "revolutionize"
- "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" / "next-generation"
- "seamless" / "robust" / "scalable" (without specifics)
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "at the end of the day"

---

## TONE BY CONTEXT

### Website copy
Confident, not boastful. States capabilities plainly. No superlatives. CTA is a suggestion, not a demand.

Good: "We map your workflow and show where automation creates leverage. No commitment."
Bad: "Transform your business with our revolutionary AI solutions today!"

### Blog posts
Technical and honest. Shows the failure modes, not just the wins. Attributes claims to specific experiences, not "research shows." No conclusions that weren't earned by the preceding paragraphs.

### Social posts (X, LinkedIn)
Short paragraphs, no bullet points. 3 hashtags max. Never sign with Mohamed's name — use "— MAW Labs" or no signature. Reads like a build journal, not a marketing post.

### Outreach emails
One ask per email. States why we're reaching out in sentence 1. No flattery. No "I came across your profile and was really impressed."

## WHAT WE ARE NOT

- We are not a "consulting firm"
- We are not an "agency"
- We are not "passionate about technology"
- We are not offering to "partner with you on your digital transformation journey"

We are an engineering company. We build specific systems that solve specific problems and deliver measurable results.

## BRAND CLAIMS WE CAN MAKE (with evidence)

- "Fixed-scope engagements — no surprise invoices" ✓ (this is our actual model)
- "Every engagement starts with a free written AI audit" ✓ (this is our actual offer)
- "We built CramRocket — launched July 2026, paying users in 30 days" ✓ (verifiable)

Do not claim things that aren't verifiable from actual shipped work.
