Most businesses we talk to have tried AI. They've played with ChatGPT, maybe bought a few tools. What they haven't done is put someone in the room who understands both the technology and the operations well enough to say: "here is exactly where AI creates leverage in your workflow, and here is what it would cost."

That's what the audit does.

## What we're looking for

When we ask about your workflow, we're not asking in a vague "tell me about your business" way. We're looking for three specific things:

**Volume + repetition.** If a task happens more than 20 times a week and follows a predictable pattern, it's a candidate for automation. Document review, data extraction, email triage, report generation — these are obvious examples. Less obvious: QA checks, customer support routing, lead scoring.

**Bottlenecks that slow decisions.** Sometimes the win isn't eliminating a task — it's cutting the time-to-decision. A system that summarizes 50-page contracts in 90 seconds doesn't replace your lawyer, but it means your lawyer spends 20 minutes on a contract instead of 2 hours.

**Data you're not using.** Most businesses generate more structured data than they analyze. Call logs, support tickets, sales notes — this data is sitting in CRMs and spreadsheets, telling you things you aren't hearing. AI makes it audible.

## The 30-minute call

We schedule a call within one business day of your inquiry. It runs 30 minutes, sometimes 45.

The call has three parts. The first 10 minutes are us asking questions — what does your team do all day, where does work pile up, what do you spend money on that feels inefficient. The next 10 minutes are us asking clarifying questions about your existing tools (what's in your stack, what's already connected to what). The final 10 minutes are us reflecting back what we heard and flagging the 2-3 areas that seem highest-value.

We don't pitch during the call. That's not the point of it. The point is to understand your operation well enough to write something useful.

## The written report

Within 3 business days of the call, you get a written report. Not a slide deck — a document.

It covers:

- The 2-3 automation opportunities we identified, ranked by estimated ROI
- A plain-language explanation of how each would work technically
- Rough implementation timelines (weeks, not months for most)
- A cost estimate range for each

The report is yours regardless of whether we work together. Most people find it useful even if they go build something themselves or hire someone else to do it.

## What happens after

If the report identifies something you want to build, we'll send a fixed-scope proposal within 48 hours. Fixed scope means a defined deliverable, a defined timeline, and a defined price — no hourly billing, no scope creep, no surprise invoices.

If nothing in the report fits your budget or timeline right now, we'll tell you what to watch for and when it might make sense to revisit. Some clients come back 6 months later when their scale justifies it.

Either outcome is fine with us. We'd rather give you an honest assessment than close a deal that doesn't make sense.
